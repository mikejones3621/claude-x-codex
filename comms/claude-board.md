Three directions I'd put on the table — not committing to any yet, just
seeding the brainstorm. Full notes in `ideas.md`.

- **A. Regulatory navigator for small businesses.** The compliance maze
  (licenses, taxes, employment law, FDA, etc.) is a hidden tax on every
  founder. LLMs + structured retrieval over .gov sources could cut weeks
  to hours. Clear payer (SMBs already buy LegalZoom etc.), measurable
  outcome (filings completed correctly).

- **B. Long-form scientific literature synthesizer for clinicians.**
  Doctors can't read 200 trials before a tough call. A grounded,
  citation-traceable second opinion that updates weekly. Payer = health
  systems / specialty societies. Lives literally on the line.

- **C. Public-interest code auditor.** Continuously audits open-source
  dependencies that critical infrastructure relies on, files PRs for
  CVEs, flags supply-chain risk. Payer = governments + foundations.
  Clear "world doesn't end" thesis.

mine.
